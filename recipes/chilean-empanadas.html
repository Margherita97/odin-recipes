Chilean empanadas

About chilean empanadas

Empanadas are a traditional recipe shared by different South American countries. Indeed, they can be found throughout Latina America, although with different names, fillings and shapes.

Today, we're going to learn how to make Chilean empanadas. Chilean empanadas usually have a rectangular or triangular shape, can be filled with different ingredients - from cheese to meat - and can be either fried or baked.

Our recipe presents the most typical Chilean empanada: empanada de pino, in its baked version.

Ingredients (15 servings)

For the filling:

  • Ground beef 500 g
  • Onions 2
  • Oil 3 tbsp
  • Cumin 1/2 tsp
  • Sugar 1/2 tsp
  • Red pepper 1 tbsp
  • Salt and pepper
  • All-purpose flour 2 tbsp
  • Black olives 20
  • Raisins 100 g
  • Hard boiled eggs 4

For the dough:

  • Vegetable shortening 200 g
  • All-purpose flour 1 kg
  • Salt 1 tbsp
  • Milk (lukewarm) 1 cup
  • Water (lukewarm) 1 cup

As finisher:

  • 1 yolk mixed with a tbsp of milk (to brush the empanadas before baking)

Directions

  1. For the pino (always prepare the day before):

    2. In a frying pan or pot, heat the oil and fry the diced onions for approximately 5 minutes. Then add the meat and immediately the seasonings (red pepper, salt, pepper, cumin) and add the raisins. Let it cook for a couple of minutes and then to thicken the pine, add the tablespoon of flour dissolved in water. Reserve.

  2. For the dough:

    3. In a bowl, put the flour together with the salt and make a well in the center. Add the melted butter, mix a little and then add the warm milk and, as necessary, add the warm water. Form a soft dough. Then, on a smooth and lightly floured surface, knead the mixture for a few minutes so that it becomes a soft and elastic dough. Divide the dough into 15 equal portions and roll each one into a round shape.

  3. Preparation of the filling and baking:

    1. Brush the edges of the dough with water. Fill with 2 to 3 tablespoons of pine, 1 olive, 1 egg tower. Fold the dough in half over the filling and press lightly to seal the edges.
    2. Brush the empanada on top with the yolk and milk mixture. And prick in the center of each empanada with the help of a toothpick.
    3. Bake at 350° F (180°C) for about 25 minutes or until golden.
    4. Your empanadas are ready! Serve them hot. Enjoy!
